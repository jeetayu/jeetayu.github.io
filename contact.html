Biography

Jeetayu (Jeet) Biswas is a physician-scientist and Hematology-Oncology Fellow at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center, training under Dr. Omar Abdel-Wahab on the Leukemia Service. He is also a Visiting Scientist at Rockefeller University, co-mentored by Dr. Ekaterina Vinogradova. His laboratory work centers on splicing factor mutations in myeloid malignancies (AML, MDS), RNA-binding protein biology, covalent chemical proteomics and activity-based protein profiling, and the development of next-generation sequencing methods including HyperTRIBE for transcriptome-wide RBP target identification.

He earned his M.D. and Ph.D. from Albert Einstein College of Medicine, where his doctoral dissertation on the sequence recognition, structure, and function of the IMP family of RNA-binding proteins was supervised by Dr. Robert H. Singer. He subsequently completed Internal Medicine residency at New York Presbyterian–Weill Cornell Medical Center before joining MSKCC for fellowship training.

His work is supported by the Leukemia and Lymphoma Society Career Development Program Fellowship, the Lauri Strauss Leukemia Foundation Research Discovery Grant, and a prior NIH/NCI F30 Ruth L. Kirschstein National Research Service Award.

Research Interests

  • RNA-binding protein target identification (HyperTRIBE / ADAR-based methods)
  • RNA splicing factor mutations in myeloid malignancies (SF3B1, SRSF2, U2AF1)
  • Covalent chemical biology & ABPP in hematopoietic cells
  • Spatial transcriptomics (Xenium) in AML and MDS
  • Isoform-specific CRISPR targeting (isoviz / Cas13d gRNA design)
