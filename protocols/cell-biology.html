Cell Freezing

Method 1 — FBS + 10% DMSO (Adherent Cells; Dry Ice)

  1. Wash cells 2× with 10 mL PBS
  2. Trypsinize 3 mL, 1 min at 37°C; quench with 10 mL DMEM + 10% FBS
  3. Transfer to 50 mL falcon; count on hemocytometer
  4. Spin; remove media
  5. Prepare 5 mL FBS + 10% DMSO (while spinning)
  6. Resuspend at ~1 million cells/mL; aliquot 1 mL per cryovial
  7. Place in Styrofoam box on dry ice: 1h → transfer to -80°C overnight → move to LN₂

Method 2 — BamBanker (Suspension Cells; Simpler)

  1. Spin 500 × g, 5 min; remove media
  2. Resuspend 2–10 million cells/mL in BamBanker
  3. Aliquot 1 mL per cryovial; place directly at -80°C
MOLM13 and K562: use BamBanker. MCF7: BamBanker, 1 million cells/mL. PDX: BamBanker, place directly at -80.

Cell Thawing

Adherent Lines (MEF, HEK, U2OS)

  1. Pre-warm media at 37°C ≥30 min before thawing
  2. Thaw in 37°C water bath until a small amount of ice remains
  3. Transfer to TC hood; mix cells with 1.5 mL media; add to 10 mL falcon with pre-warmed media
  4. Spin 5 min; remove supernatant (to remove DMSO)
  5. Resuspend in fresh media; plate on 10 cm dish
  6. Check adherence after 1–2 h

Suspension Lines (MOLM13, K562, THP1)

  1. Thaw in water bath; as soon as liquid, transfer to 10 mL pre-warmed media in 15 mL falcon
  2. Spin 400–500 × g, 5 min; remove supernatant
  3. Resuspend in fresh media; plate in appropriate vessel

MCF7

Thaw; add immediately to 10 mL warm media; plate on 10 cm dish. Leave 1–2 days before media change.

Transfection — FuGENE HD

Use Transfection Calculator for scaling. Ratio 2:1 (reagent:DNA) for standard; 3:1 for large constructs.

10 cm Dish (10 µg DNA)

  1. 10 µg DNA into 1.5 mL tube; add 470 µL PBS + 20 µL FuGENE HD
  2. Flick vigorously; rest 10–20 min at RT
  3. Add dropwise to cells

3.5 cm Dish

2 µg DNA + 3 µL FuGENE HD in 100 µL total

Transfection — PEI (HEK293T, 10 cm Dish)

PEI stock: 1 mg/mL in H₂O (pH with HCl; filter sterilize). Ratio 4:1 (PEI:DNA by mass).

  1. Tube A: 10 µg DNA in 500 µL Opti-MEM
  2. Tube B: 500 µL Opti-MEM + 40 µL PEI (1 mg/mL)
  3. Add Tube A into Tube B; mix by pipetting; incubate 10 min at RT
  4. Aspirate media from cells; add PEI-DNA complex
  5. Incubate 2–4 h; add complete media; assess GFP next day
For 6-well optimization: test PEI:DNA ratios 0:1, 1:2, 1:4, 1:6, 1:8 with 2 µg DNA per well.

Transfection — Calcium Phosphate (HEK293T)

Reagents

  • 2X HBS: 50 mM HEPES, 280 mM NaCl, 1.5 mM Na₂HPO₄, pH 7.0 (adjust with HCl); sterile filter
  • 2 M CaCl₂: 11.1 g per 50 mL; sterile filter
  • 0.1× TE buffer: sterile filtered

6-well Dish (2–3 µg DNA per well)

  1. Switch cells to antibiotic-free media a few hours before transfection
  2. Add DNA to 90 µL 0.1× TE; add 10 µL 2M CaCl₂; mix 5 min at RT
  3. Add 100 µL 2× HBS dropwise while vortexing
  4. Rest 20 min at RT (slightly cloudy = good)
  5. Add to cells; incubate overnight
  6. Replace with fresh complete DMEM

10 cm dish (5× scale): 10–20 µg DNA in 450 µL 0.1× TE + 50 µL 2 M CaCl₂ + 500 µL 2× HBS.

Transfection — Nucleoporation (MEFs)

  • 100 µL transfection reagent (Lonza SE buffer or equivalent)
  • 10 µg plasmid DNA per reaction
  • Program A024 (for MEFs)
  • Immediately transfer to pre-warmed media; do not centrifuge for first 24 h

Lentiviral Packaging & K562 Spinfection

Packaging (3rd Generation, 10 cm dish)

  • Co-transfect HEK293T with: transfer vector + GAG/POL + REV + VSV-G using Lipofectamine
  • Collect media at 48h and 72h; filter 0.45 µm; concentrate with Lenti-X precipitation

K562 Spinfection

  1. JetOptimus transfection of packaging cells; collect virus 48 h post-transfection
  2. Spin K562 cells onto virus: 2300 rpm, 32°C, 90 min
  3. Polybrene: 4 µg/mL
  4. Recover in fresh media; select with puromycin after 48 h

RNA Isolation — TRIzol (MatTek Dish or 10 cm Dish)

  1. Wash dish with 2 mL cold PBS
  2. Add 200 µL TRIzol (Tri-Reagent); pipette up and down to dissociate
  3. Transfer to Eppendorf; add 40 µL chloroform; shake 15 s; incubate RT 2–3 min
  4. Centrifuge 12,000 × g, 15 min, 4°C; remove aqueous phase (top layer) — avoid white DNA layer
  5. Add 1 µL glycogen; add 100 µL isopropanol; incubate RT 10 min
  6. Spin 12,000 × g, 10 min, 4°C; pellet is clear
  7. Wash 200 µL 75% EtOH; spin 7,500 × g, 5 min; remove EtOH; air dry RT 10 min
  8. Resuspend in TE buffer (≥40 µL); quantify on Nanodrop

DNase Digest

1–2 µg RNA + 4 µL 10× Turbo DNase buffer + 1 µL Turbo DNase → 40 µL total; 37°C, 30 min. Add 4 µL inactivation resin; incubate; centrifuge; transfer RNA.

Mouse Hemisphere

  1. Add 500 µL TRIzol; pipette up/down; pass 5× through 22.5G needle
  2. RT 5–15 min; spin 10,000 × g, 5 min; transfer supernatant
  3. Add 100 µL chloroform → proceed as above but incubate isopropanol overnight at -20°C
Typical yields: U2OS/K562 (10 cm dish) ~260 ng/µL in 40 µL; MBS neurons (MatTek) ~20–30 ng/µL.

CD34+ Isolation — MACS (Miltenyi)

  1. Ficoll enrichment: layer blood over Ficoll in SepMate tube; spin 400 × g, 35 min
  2. Collect mononuclear layer; wash 2× PBS; count cells
  3. Block FcR: 10 µL FcR blocking reagent per 10⁷ cells; 10 min at 4°C
  4. Add CD34 MicroBeads: 100 µL per 10⁸ cells; 30 min at 4°C
  5. Wash; resuspend in MACS buffer; load onto LS column in QuadroMACS separator
  6. Wash 3× with MACS buffer; remove column from magnet; elute
  7. Spin 400 × g, 5 min to collect; count; proceed to culture or flash freeze
CD45+ isolation: identical workflow with CD45 MicroBeads. Used for PDX mouse bone marrows and spleens.

PDX — Bone Marrow Harvest & Engraftment

Bone Marrow Harvest (mortar/pestle method)

  1. Sacrifice mouse; collect femurs, tibias, pelvis
  2. Crush bones in mortar; strain through 40 µm filter into PBS
  3. ACK lysis (1 mL per 10⁷ cells, 2 min RT); quench with PBS; spin 400 × g, 5 min
  4. Count; proceed to MACS or culture

Spleen Processing

Mash through 40 µm filter; ACK lysis; spin 400 × g, 5 min; resuspend in media.

Tail Vein Injection

  • 1 million cells in 100 µL per NSG mouse
  • Strain through 40 µm before injection; count and confirm viability
  • Pre-warm tail in warm water 1–2 min to dilate veins

Flow Cytometry — Surface Staining & Sorting

Sorting Buffer Recipes

BufferComponents
Basic FACS bufferPBS + 2 mM EDTA + 25 mM HEPES + 2% FCS
MEF sorting bufferAbove + sodium azide + DAPI (1 µg/mL for live/dead)

Cell Surface Staining

  1. Spin cells; resuspend in 50–100 µL FACS buffer per sample
  2. Add antibody (typically 1:200–1:1000 dilution); mix; 30 min on ice in dark
  3. Wash 2× with 2 mL FACS buffer; spin 400 × g, 3 min
  4. Resuspend in 200–500 µL buffer + live/dead stain; acquire

Live/Dead Stains

  • PI (propidium iodide): 1 µg/mL, add immediately before acquisition
  • DAPI: 1 µg/mL in non-fixed cells
  • 7-AAD: 0.25 µg per 10⁶ cells; 10 min RT; wash

Viability Assays

CellTiter-Glo (384-well)

  1. Equilibrate reagent and plates to RT 30 min
  2. Add 25 µL CellTiter-Glo to 50 µL cells in 384-well white plate
  3. Shake 2 min; RT 15 min
  4. Read luminescence

Caspase 3/7 Assay (Flow Cytometry)

  • 96-well U-bottom plate; 500 × g spins throughout
  • Components A/B per kit instructions; 30 min RT in dark
  • Wash; resuspend in FACS buffer + PI; acquire

Tecan D300 Drug Printer

  • 10 mM DMSO compound stocks in T8 cassette
  • Target DMSO: 0.1% final
  • Use D3003 software for dispensing layout

Immunofluorescence Staining

Fixation Methods

  • PFA (4%): 15 min RT; quench overnight in 4°C glycine (0.1 M in PBS)
  • MeOH (ice cold): 10 min on ice; better for lysosome morphology

Standard IF Protocol

  1. Wash 3× PBS; permeabilize 5 min (0.1% Triton X-100 in PBS, PFA-fixed only)
  2. Block 30 min: 5% BSA or 5% donkey serum in PBS
  3. Primary antibody in block; 1–2h RT or overnight 4°C
  4. Wash 3× PBS; secondary antibody in block; 1h RT in dark
  5. Wash 3× PBS; DAPI 1:10,000 in PBS, 5 min; wash; mount

Coverslip Preparation

  • Fibronectin: 10 µg/mL; 0.8 mL per well (12-well); 37°C, 30 min; aspirate; air dry
  • Poly-D-Lysine: 50 µg/mL; 1 mL per well; 37°C, 1h; wash 2× PBS; air dry
  • Plasma cleaning: 3 min in plasma cleaner before coating for best adhesion

Organelle & Fusion-Tag Dye Labeling

Lysotracker

  • Stock: 1 mM in DMSO (Cuervo lab stock)
  • Working: 1:10,000 → 100 nM or 1:20,000 → 50 nM in serum-free media
  • Incubate 20–30 min at 37°C; wash; image live

MitoTracker

  • Stock: 1 mM in DMSO
  • Working: 25 nM (dense cultures) or 5 nM (sparse) in serum-free media
  • 30 min at 37°C; wash; proceed to fixation or live imaging

JF549 Halo Dye Labeling

  • Stock: 5 µM; Working: 1:6,000 → ~0.83 nM
  • 15 min at 37°C; wash 3× media; image

ABPP — Activity Based Protein Profiling

See Full Protocol Book for complete V1 (short, IP then gel) and V2 (5-day, overexpression) workflows. Key steps below.

General ABPP Workflow

  1. Lysis: Sonication in PBS + protease inhibitors (3 min, 1400 × g spin)
  2. Normalize: DC assay → dilute to 1 mg/mL; use 200 µg (200 µL) per sample
  3. Scout compound: 2 µL of 100× stock; 1h RT (e.g. KB05 at 500 µM final)
  4. Alkyne probe: 2 µL of 100× stock; 1h RT (e.g. KB05-yne at 100 µM final)
  5. FLAG-IP: dilute 1:1.5 with 0.1% Triton-X/PBS; add 30 µL anti-FLAG M2 beads; rotate 2h 4°C; wash 2× IP buffer + 3× PBS
  6. Click reaction (fresh TCEP each time): 1 µL CuSO₄ [50 mM] + 3 µL TBTA [1.7 mM] + 1 µL TCEP [50 mM] + 1 µL Rh-azide; incubate 1h RT
  7. Gel: quench 30 µL LDS; boil 95°C 7 min; SDS-PAGE; image on Bio-Rad ChemiDoc (DyLight 550 for Rh-N₃)
Compounds stock: KB02, KB05, KB02-yne, KB05-yne in DMSO. TCEP: make fresh each time (50 mM in PBS). TBTA: 1.7 mM in 4:1 tBuOH:DMSO.
